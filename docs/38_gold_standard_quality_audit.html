ExECTv2 Quality Audit — 11 May 2026

The Gold Standard Is Clinically Informed But Structurally Loose

An exhaustive, evidence-based audit of 200 synthetic epilepsy clinic letters revealing a systematic measurement bias that punishes clinical accuracy and rewards extraction breadth.

Fonferko-Shadrach et al. (2024) ExECTv2 Corpus Manual Review v9 Guidelines
Explore the audit
Executive Summary

Eight Critical Findings

The central finding is that the gold standard is misaligned with the temporally aware, precision-oriented extraction system this project has built. The misalignment is not a minor implementation detail; it is a systematic measurement bias.

Critical
01

Temporally Flat by Construction

Seizure types are harvested from MarkupSeizureFrequency.csv without temporal filtering. Historical seizure types from 2014, 2016, and 2017 are treated as current patient findings. A patient who is explicitly seizure-free today is gold-labelled with their historical seizure types.

Critical
02

Guidelines Sanction Temporal Blindness

Example 4 and Example 5 in the Diagnosis section instruct annotators to label negated/historical seizure mentions as affirmed diagnoses because "the history of having GTCS is affirmed." The guideline explicitly teaches annotators to ignore temporal scope.

High
03

Inter-Annotator Agreement Only F1 = 0.73

Two trained human annotators disagree on more than one in four annotations. Prescriptions scored highest (0.87); "When Diagnosed" lowest (0.45).

High
04

Systematic Span Boundary Noise

37 trailing-hyphen spans, 9 truncated endings, and 3 mid-word starts found in just 20 documents. Boundary defects degrade exact-span training quality.

High
05

Duplicate and Conflicting Annotations

Seven spans carry contradictory attributes. Medication annotations include dose conflicts (sodium valproate labelled as both 700 mg and 800 mg on overlapping spans).

Moderate
06

Generic Seizure Terms Pollute the Label Set

MarkupSeizureFrequency.csv contains 111 entries where the seizure-type field is seizures, seizure, or null. These reveal a lack of type-level precision in source annotations.

Critical
07

Extraction Systems Are More Precise Than Gold Allows

MA_v1 correctly identifies EA0006 is seizure-free and excludes 2014 historical types; it scores F1 = 0.0. A broken pipeline that ignores temporality scores F1 = 1.0. This inversion is the defining symptom of a measurement problem, not a model problem.

High
08

The Oracle Failure Rate Is Partly an Artefact of Annotation Looseness

Many "failures" occur because the model outputs unknown seizure type or seizure free while the gold expects a historical-specific type, or because the model correctly abstains where the gold incorrectly infers.

Bottom line: The seizure-type F1 ceiling of ~0.60–0.65 on validation is not primarily a model-capacity ceiling; it is a gold-standard fidelity ceiling. The way forward is not more prompt engineering. It is better measurement.

The Smoking Gun

Guidelines That Teach Temporal Blindness

The v9 guidelines are detailed, pragmatic, and critically, they contain explicit examples that teach annotators to ignore temporal scope for seizure types and to treat historical mentions as current diagnoses.

“He has not had a generalised tonic clonic seizure for a while.”

Diagnosis: generalised tonic clonic seizure, Certainty = 5, Negation = Affirmed

“Although he is not having seizures now, he 'usually' does... it states that he has had gtcs.”

“I was pleased to hear that she has not had any further generalised tonic clonic seizures since August 2016”

Diagnosis: generalised tonic clonic seizure, Certainty = 5, Negation = Affirmed

“Although the statement is negated the history of having generalised tonic clonic seizures is affirmed, so it should be annotated.”

Impact: When MA_v1 correctly segments EA0006 as "seizure free" and excludes the 2014 GTCS and absence-like seizures, it is behaving against the guideline's taught behavior. The pipeline's clinically correct output scores F1 = 0.0.

Construction Logic

How evaluate.py Builds the Target

Understanding how the scorer constructs its gold label sets is essential, because the construction logic itself introduces distortions beyond those present in the raw annotations.

Seizure Types: Unconditional Harvest

# src/evaluate.py, in load_gold() for row in read_csv_rows(markup_root / "MarkupSeizureFrequency.csv"): ... seizure_type = canonical_seizure_type(row[5] if row[5].lower() != "null" else row[4]) ... if seizure_type: document.seizure_types.append(seizure_type)

The seizure_types list is populated from MarkupSeizureFrequency.csv, not from MarkupDiagnosis.csv. Every row with a non-empty seizure-type value appends that type to the document's list regardless of temporal scope.

Medications: All Rows Treated as Current

for row in read_csv_rows(markup_root / "MarkupPrescriptions.csv"): document.medications.append({ "name": canonical_medication_name(row[4] if row[4].lower() != "null" else row[5]), "dose": normalize_dose(row[6]), ... })

The Prescriptions CSV is loaded without any temporal filter. The guidelines say "only current prescriptions," but the scorer does not verify this.

Investigations: Simple Keyword Matching

phrase = normalize_value(row[4]) result = "abnormal" if "abnormal" in phrase else "normal" if "normal" in phrase else None if "eeg" in phrase and result: document.investigations["eeg"] = result

The investigation loader does not use structured MRI_Results / EEG_Results columns. It keyword-matches the normalized phrase string. It cannot distinguish "abnormal EEG" from "no abnormal EEG."

Evidence

Per-Category Case Studies

Twenty-five documents representing every major failure mode, cross-referenced against system outputs to identify where clinically correct predictions are scored as false negatives.

EA0006
The Paradox Document
“I was pleased to hear that he remains seizure free and is now driving.”

Gold: generalized tonic clonic seizure (×2), generalized absence seizure. Clinical reality: seizure-free since 2014. MA_v1 output: ['seizure free']. F1 = 0.0. The broken pipeline scores F1 = 1.0.

EA0004
Genuine Success
“Seizure taking frequency: Uncertain, several seizures since the last clinic appointment”

Gold: unknown seizure type (×2). MA_v1 output: ['unknown seizure type']. F1 = 1.0. This success is masked at the aggregate level by EA0006-type regressions.

EA0011
Historical Secondary Generalization
“Focal to bilateral convulsive seizures, last event around Christmas 2017... his last one being around Christmas time in 2017”

Gold: focal seizure, focal to bilateral convulsive seizure. Last convulsive seizure was 2017. The convulsive subtype is historical, yet the gold labels it current. F1 = 1.0 reflects matching a flat label set, not clinical extraction.

EA0005
Ambiguous Temporal Scope
“Generalised tonic clonic seizure-last event July 2016... he has had roughly two seizures per year since then.”

Gold: generalized tonic clonic seizure. The letter mentions "last event July 2016" but also "two seizures per year since 2010." The gold's flat label set cannot represent this nuance. A temporally aware system faces an impossible target.

Aggregate: The Meta-Label Ceiling
Structural Benchmark Problem

Across all models (4B to 35B) and all harnesses, the miss count for unknown seizure type is consistently 13–15 out of 26 documents that have this gold label. Scale does not close this gap. The gold uses unknown seizure type as a catch-all for generic mentions, but guidelines do not clearly define when to use it. Models infer specific types from context, which is clinically correct but benchmark-incorrect.

EA0017
The null Drug Name

EA0017.txt,229,255,C0016410,"null","folic-acid",5,mg,1,...

The canonical DrugName field is null; the surface text is folic-acid. But folic acid is not an anti-seizure medication. The guidelines say "only current prescriptions for ASMs." Its inclusion is an annotator error. Our systems correctly exclude it, creating a false negative on the gold label.

EA0183
Conflicting Doses

Two overlapping Prescription labels on "Sodium-Valproate", one with DrugDose: 800 and the other with DrugDose: 700.

A system predicting sodium valproate | 800 | mg | 2 would match one gold tuple but miss the other, producing a false negative (700) and a true positive (800). The system's precision is penalized for a gold-standard inconsistency.

EA0195
Tegretol Dose Ambiguity

Overlapping Tegretol labels: "Tegretol-" with DrugDose: 600, while "Tegretol-400mg-in-the-morning" has DrugDose: 400.

This may reflect total daily dose (600 mg) versus per-dose amount (400 mg morning). The JSON/CSV does not distinguish these concepts. A system predicting carbamazepine | 400 | mg | 2 would miss the 600 mg tuple.

EA0188
Misspelled Drug Name

"Brivitiracetam" with DrugName: "brivitiracetam" but CUIPhrase: "brivaracetam".

The surface text contains a misspelling. Our canonical_medication_name() normalizer maps brivitiracetam → brivaracetam (via ASM_SYNONYMS). Without that repair, the original scorer would have produced a false negative. The gold standard required scorer-side normalization repair to be scoreable at all.

EA0002
Dual Diagnosis Overlap

"focal-epilepsy" (certainty 5) and "epilepsy-Probable-temporal" (certainty 4) on overlapping spans.

Two overlapping spans annotate the same clinical concept with different specificity and different certainty. The first covers the definite part. The second covers the probable localization. Our scorer maps both to focal epilepsy. But a system predicting temporal lobe epilepsy might not match, even though the letter explicitly says "probably temporal."

EA0006
Certainty = 3 for Generalised Epilepsy
“Diagnosis: Epilepsy – unclassified, possibly generalised.”

The word "possibly" triggers certainty = 3. Our systems, prompted to extract only clear diagnoses, might exclude this. The gold includes it because Negation = Affirmed. The scorer ignores certainty. A precision-oriented system is penalized for correctly excluding a tentative diagnosis.

EA0003
Generic Seizures as Diagnosis

"generalised-seizures" annotated as DiagCategory = MultipleSeizures, not Epilepsy.

The term "generalised seizures" is generic — it does not specify tonic-clonic, absence, or myoclonic. The ILAE classification would not accept it as a specific type. Yet it appears in the gold seizure-type set via the frequency CSV. Our canonical_seizure_type() maps it to generalized seizure, which is acceptable at the benchmark level but imprecise clinically.

EA0194
Direct Contradiction

Same span annotated twice: NumberOfSeizures = 0 and UpperNumberOfSeizures = 1, TimePeriod = Year.

These cannot both be true for the same clinical assertion. The presence of contradictory gold labels makes correct extraction statistically impossible.

EA0069
Dual Interpretation

Same span: FrequencyChange = Increased and NumberOfSeizures = 4, TimePeriod = Week.

Not contradictory — complementary. One captures change direction; the other captures rate. But the scorer expects a single current_seizure_frequency.value string. A system predicting "4 per week" would miss "Increased."

EA0070
Qualitative vs Quantitative Split

Same span: FrequencyChange = Infrequent and UpperNumberOfSeizures = 1, TimePeriod = Year.

Our pragmatic scorer maps "1 per year" to infrequent and scores a match. But the strict scorer requires exact string or parts match. The dual annotation creates a split target.

EA0010
Seizure-Free Duration Encoding

NumberOfSeizures = 0, with AgeLower = 13, AgeUpper = 19, AgeUnit = Year.

This represents "seizure-free for 13–19 years." The gold loader constructs the string "0 per 13 year", which is clinically nonsensical. The underlying annotation structure (using Age fields for seizure-free duration) is semantically overloaded.

EA0022
The 68-Character EEG Span

"EEG-she-had-some-of-these-episodes-and-there-was-no-epileptiform-EEG"

The annotated span is 68 characters long and contains the full sentence. "No epileptiform EEG" is negated, but the result is annotated as Normal — clinically correct. Yet the span includes a great deal of irrelevant text. A system extracting evidence quotes would produce a much shorter span, failing quote-validity checks.

Aggregate
EEG Type Unpopulated

Of 182 investigation annotations, 104 have EEG_Type = null (57%). Only 78 have any value, and the majority of those are Yes (indicating EEG_Performed) rather than a specific type. The guidelines state type should be annotated "if stated (it should not be assumed)." The low population rate suggests most letters do not state the EEG type, or annotators missed it.

EA0188
Severe Span Defects

Prior inspection found "eiz" as a PatientHistory span (mid-word truncation), "ondary-generalised-seiz" as a Diagnosis span (mid-word start and end), "drug-refractory" mapped to CUIPhrase "drug", and "occipital-lobe)-epilepsy" mapped to CUIPhrase "Occipital". These are not minor formatting issues. A model trained on these spans would learn to output partial tokens.

EA0198
Conflicting Onset Ages

Two Onset labels on the same "epilepsy" span: one says onset age 23 years, another says 15 years. This may reflect two different clinical events (first seizure at 15, diagnosis at 23), but the annotation does not distinguish them. A system predicting either age would score a false negative on the other.

EA0184
Truncated Absence Spans

"typical-absenc" and "typical-absen" for Diagnosis and SeizureFrequency, both truncated rather than the full phrase "typical absences". Truncated spans degrade exact-span training and evaluation.

EA0006
Family History Is Invisible
“There is no family history of epilepsy and both his sons are well and have not had seizures.”

No family history annotations exist (the category is not in the schema). A single-pass system might extract "epilepsy" and "seizures" from this sentence and incorrectly include them as patient findings. Fixing the family-history trap provides zero score improvement. The only visible effect is the temporal fix, which lowers the score.

Normalization

When Concepts Lose Their Meaning

The CUIPhrase field is supposed to normalize surface text to a canonical UMLS concept. In practice, it frequently strips critical clinical specificity.

generalised-tonic-clonic-seizures generalised
drug-refractory drug
occipital-lobe)-epilepsy Occipital
focal-motor-seizures focal
ischaemic-damage brain
transient-loss-of-consciousness Transient

The project's canonical_seizure_type() and canonical_diagnosis() normalizers override many of these issues by mapping to benchmark categories. But this repair is performed in the scorer, not in the gold data. The original scorer (before Phase 2/3) produced much lower scores because it did not have these normalizations.

The Core Argument

Precision vs. Looseness

When a precise system meets a loose gold standard, the result is systematic under-scoring of correct behavior.

Our Systems: Clinically Precise
  • ✓ Distinguish current from historical findings
  • ✓ Distinguish patient from family history
  • ✓ Require evidence quotes for every extraction
  • ✓ Normalize to specific, clinically meaningful categories
  • ✓ Abstain when text does not support inference
Gold Standard: Clinically Loose
  • ✗ Treats historical seizure types as current diagnoses
  • ✗ Has no family-history field
  • ✗ Does not require evidence spans to be clinically meaningful
  • ✗ Uses broad, sometimes contradictory categories
  • ✗ Penalizes abstention

The "best" scores come from systems that extract broadly (S2, E3). Systems that try to be precise (H7, D3, MA_v1) score lower or become unstable. This is not because they extract worse clinically; it is because they extract more carefully than the gold standard allows.

The Asymmetry Problem

The evaluation protocol is asymmetric. It can punish precision but cannot reward it. A system that fixes both temporal bleeding and family-history bleeding looks worse than a system that fixes neither.

↓

Punishes Temporal Precision

Excluding historical seizures lowers F1. The gold expects 2014 events as "current."

—

Blind to Family-History Precision

Excluding family-history seizures has zero score effect. No field exists to reward this.

↓

Penalizes Abstention

Outputting unknown seizure type is clinically safe but benchmark-penalized.

—

Ignores Evidence Quality

Quote validity (≥0.96) does not feed the benchmark composite score.

This is the opposite of what a clinical evaluation should do. A system that fixes both temporal bleeding and family-history bleeding looks worse than a system that fixes neither.

The Path Forward

Recommendations

01
Immediate

Do not promote or demote systems based on seizure-type F1 alone

The metric is confounded by gold-standard temporal blindness. Use benchmark composite as a guide, but weigh medication and diagnosis more heavily.

02
Immediate

Run a temporal-scope-aware scorer audit on the full validation split

For each document, manually tag whether each gold seizure type is current, historical, or unclear. Compute seizure_type_f1_collapsed_current and seizure_type_f1_collapsed_all. Quantify the bias.

03
Immediate

Report dual metrics for seizure type in the dissertation

Report both seizure_type_f1_collapsed_all (backward-compatible with literature) and seizure_type_f1_collapsed_current (the clinically meaningful metric). If MA_v1 or D3 outperforms on the current-only metric but underperforms on the all-mentions metric, frame this as a positive finding about temporal precision, not a failure.

04
Immediate

Fix the Stage 3 verifier prompt

Preserve seizure free and unknown seizure type meta-labels. This is a genuine bug fix (already done in src/multi_agent.py).

05
Medium-Term

Reframe the dissertation claim on multi-agent decomposition

Instead of "multi-agent decomposition improves overall extraction quality," the supported claim is: "Multi-agent decomposition improves temporal precision and robustness to context bleeding, with mixed effects on aggregate F1 due to gold-standard temporal blindness. This trade-off is clinically desirable: a system that correctly reports a seizure-free patient is more useful than one that correctly matches a flat label set."

06
Medium-Term

Quantify the precision-looseness gap explicitly

Add a section to the dissertation methods titled "Gold-Standard Fidelity and the Precision-Looseness Tension," citing this audit and the prior inspection. This is a genuine contribution to clinical NLP methodology.

07
Long-Term

Propose a temporally structured extraction benchmark

The ExECTv2 schema has temporal_scope for frequencies but not for types. Future benchmarks should require systems to report when a seizure type was observed, not just what it was.

08
Long-Term

Advocate for evidence-grounded scoring

The project's quote-validity metric (≥0.96 across all conditions) is a strong clinical safety signal. Future benchmarks should incorporate evidence overlap into primary metrics, not just as a secondary check.

Conclusion

Better Measurement, Not More Prompt Engineering

The project's true contribution is not achieving F1 = 0.76 on a noisy target. It is building extraction systems that know the difference between a seizure-free patient and a patient whose seizures were last mentioned in 2014 — and having the evidence to prove it.

The ExECTv2 gold standard is a valuable and pioneering resource. However, it is not clean enough to serve as an unexamined target for precision-oriented extraction systems. The seizure-type F1 ceiling of ~0.60–0.65 is not a model-capacity ceiling. It is a gold-standard fidelity ceiling. The way forward is temporally scoped scoring, meta-label validation, explicit precision-looseness accounting, and honest reporting of the trade-off between matching a flat label set and extracting clinically truthful information.
